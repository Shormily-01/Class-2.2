01 Cox's Bazar Stretching a remarkable 75 miles in length, Cox's Bazar Beach is one of the longest uninterrupted natural sand beaches in all of Bangladesh. Most of Cox's Bazar is 660 feet wide at high tide, and 1,300 feet wide at low tide.
02 Bandarban Among the eye-catching places, the most remote and least populated Bandarban is a popular destination for its adventurous, distinctive and scenic landscape. 03 Sundarban The Sundarbans is of universal importance for globally endangered species including the Royal Bengal Tiger, Ganges and Irawadi dolphins, estuarine crocodiles and the critically endangered endemic river terrapin (Batagur baska). It is the only mangrove habitat in the world for Panthera tigris tigris species.
